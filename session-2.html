Listening 2: Planning a Party

Tom, Lucy & Sarah plan a party together

Show Transcript
Tom: Hey, Lucy! I'm planning a party for Saturday. Can you help me with the decorations?
Lucy: Oh, I'd love to help! What do you need me to do?
Tom: Can you bring some balloons and streamers? I think they'll make the place look great.
Lucy: Sure, I can bring balloons! Do you want me to get some colorful ones?
Tom: Yes, please! And can you help me put them up at 4 p.m. on Saturday?
Lucy: Of course, I'll be there. What about food? Do you need any help with that?
Tom: I think I can handle the food. But thanks for asking!
Lucy: No problem! Let me know if you need anything else.
Sarah: Hi, everyone! I heard you're planning a party. Can I help with anything?
Tom: Hi, Sarah! Well, we need some music. Can you bring a Bluetooth speaker?
Sarah: Sure! I'll bring it. I can also help with setting up the drinks.
Tom: That would be awesome. But I think I already have enough drinks. Thanks for offering!
Sarah: No problem! Let me know if you need anything else. I'm happy to help.
Tom: Thanks, everyone. I'm excited about the party!

📝 Step 3: Grammar — The -ing Form & Offering Help

📊 Grammar Slides

1 / 12

🔑 The Golden Rule

After a preposition (by, at, for, with, of, in) the verb ALWAYS gets -ing.

Structure: preposition + verb-ing

🟢 Preposition + -ing Examples

✅ I can help by bringing the drinks.

✅ She is good at organizing parties.

✅ Thank you for helping with the decorations!

✅ Can I help with decorating the place?

✅ I will take care of buying the cake.

💬 Offering, Accepting & Refusing Help

OfferAcceptRefuse (politely)
Can I help with...?Yes, please!Thanks, but I've got it covered.
Would you like me to...?That would be great!I think I can manage, but thanks!
I'd be happy to...I'd love some help!That's kind of you, but I'm fine.

⚠ THE BIG MISTAKE

offer/refuse + to + base verb (NOT -ing!)

❌ WRONG

"I offered helping you."

"He refuses coming."

✅ CORRECT

"I offered to help you."

"He refuses to come."

Remember: offer/refuse are REBELS — they use TO!

🌟 Emma's Party Plan

Emma will have a party at her house next weekend. She will begin by planning the menu and choosing the decorations. She will make a list of everything she will need, like food, drinks, and music.

Emma will start by preparing sandwiches and pizza for her friends. She will also bake a big chocolate cake. For drinks, she will focus on buying orange juice, soda, and water. She will decorate the living room by hanging balloons and putting up a colorful banner.

Emma will send invitations to her friends on Friday. She will ask them to come at 6 p.m., and to help by bringing their favorite games to play together. Emma hopes the party will be fun for everyone.
Did you notice? The passage uses many -ing forms: planning, choosing, preparing, buying, hanging, bringing! These are all verbs after prepositions.

🎂 Role 1: The Party Planner

You are planning a big birthday party for Saturday.

  • You need help with decorations, food, music, and drinks
  • Accept some offers and politely refuse others
  • Use: "Yes, please!" / "Thanks, but I've got it covered."
You: Hey everyone! I'm planning a birthday party for Saturday. There's so much to do!
Friend: Can I help with the decorations?
You: Yes, please! Can you bring some balloons and streamers?
Friend: Sure! Would you like me to bring food too?
You: Thanks, but I've got it covered. I'm preparing sandwiches and pizza.

🙋 Role 2: The Eager Helper

You want to help your friend with their party.

  • Offer to help with at least 3 different things
  • Use -ing form: "I'm good at decorating..."
  • Accept polite refusals gracefully
You: I heard you're planning a party! Can I help with anything?
Planner: Yes! Can you help with the music?
You: Sure! I'm good at making playlists. I can also help by bringing my Bluetooth speaker.

🍕 Role 3: The Food Expert

You are great at cooking and baking.

  • Offer to help with food and drinks
  • Use: "I can help by preparing..."
  • Ask what food the planner needs
You: I'm really good at baking! Can I help by making the birthday cake?
Planner: Yes, please! That would be amazing!
You: I can also help with preparing some snacks. What do you need?

😊 Role 4: The Polite Refuser

You are organized and prefer to do most things yourself.

  • Politely refuse some offers while accepting others
  • Use: "That's kind of you, but..." / "I think I can manage!"
Friend: Would you like me to organize the music?
You: That's kind of you, but I already made a playlist.
Friend: How about the decorations?
You: Actually, yes! I'd love some help with hanging the balloons.
