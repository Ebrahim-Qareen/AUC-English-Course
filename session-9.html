💬 Idioms

Survival Idioms

Common English idioms about survival, challenges, and going with the flow. Each includes meaning, example, and Arabic hint.

⛵ In the same boat

Meaning: Being in the same difficult situation as others

Example: "We're all in the same boat — none of us speak the language."

Arabic: كلنا في نفس المركب

🏊 Sink or swim

Meaning: Either succeed or fail by your own effort

Example: "It's sink or swim — nobody is going to help us."

Arabic: يا تنجح يا تفشل بمجهودك

🌊 Weather the storm

Meaning: Survive a difficult period

Example: "The company managed to weather the storm during the crisis."

Arabic: يتحمل الصعوبات

💪 Keep your head above water

Meaning: Barely manage to survive or cope

Example: "With all these bills, I'm just trying to keep my head above water."

Arabic: بالعافية ماشي

⚓ Rock the boat

Meaning: Cause trouble or disturb a stable situation

Example: "Don't rock the boat — everything is going well."

Arabic: يعمل مشاكل / يقلب الدنيا

🌊 Go with the flow

Meaning: Accept things as they happen

Example: "I don't have a plan, I'll just go with the flow."

Arabic: يمشي مع التيار

🌡 Test the waters

Meaning: Try something carefully before committing

Example: "Let's test the waters before we invest all our money."

Arabic: يجرّب الأول

📝 Grammar

What a + Noun Phrase

📊 Grammar Slides

1 / 1

❗ What a + (adjective) + noun!

Use What a to express strong feelings about something. Pattern: What a + (adjective) + singular noun (+ subject + verb)!

PatternExample
What a + noun!What a surprise!
What a + adj + noun!What a beautiful island!
What a + adj + noun + S + V!What a scary night it was!

❗ What + (adjective) + plural/uncountable noun!

Use What (no "a") with plural or uncountable nouns.

PatternExample
What + adj + plural noun!What beautiful flowers!
What + adj + uncountable!What terrible weather!

❓ Compare with: How + adjective!

Use How + adjective (no noun after).

PatternExample
How + adjective!How beautiful!
How + adjective!How scary!
Remember: "What a" = singular nouns. "What" alone = plural/uncountable. "How" = adjectives alone (no noun).

📖 Reading

Alone on a Desert Island

Alexander Selkirk was a sailor who lived alone on an island for more than four years. In 1704, he was left on a deserted island in the Pacific Ocean called Más a Tierra (now known as Robinson Crusoe Island). The island was over 400 miles off the coast of Chile. He chose to stay there because he believed the ship he was on would sink.

Selkirk had only a few things with him: some clothes, tools, and a gun. At first, he hoped a ship would rescue him, but no one came. He realized he had to survive on his own.

The island had no people, but it had wild goats, rats, and cats. Selkirk hunted goats for food and used their skins to make clothes. He made tools to help him build a shelter. The island was full of trees, hills, and beaches. It was beautiful but lonely.

One day, Spanish ships came to the island. Selkirk ran and hid because he knew the Spanish would capture him. He stayed safe until they left.

In February 1709, British ships finally arrived. Selkirk made a fire to signal them. The sailors were shocked to find a man living alone on the island, wearing clothes made of goat skin. After four years and four months, Selkirk was rescued.

Living on the island had saved Selkirk's life. He later learned that the ship he had left had sunk, and most of the crew had died.

🎭 Roleplay

Roleplay Scenarios

🏝 Scenario 1: Stranded on an Island

  • You are stranded on a deserted island with a friend
  • Decide what to do first: find shelter, look for water, or make a fire
  • Use survival vocabulary and What a + NP

You: Oh no! What a scary situation this is! We need to find shelter first.

Friend: I agree. Look at those palm trees over there. We can use the leaves.

You: Good idea! We also need to find fresh water. We can't drink seawater.

Friend: What a beautiful island, though! Look at the beach!

You: It is beautiful, but we have to focus on survival. Let's light a fire too — it will help rescuers find us.

Friend: You're right. What a brave person you are!

📰 Scenario 2: Rescued After 6 Months

  • You were rescued after 6 months on a deserted island
  • A reporter is interviewing you about your experience
  • Tell your story using past tense and What a + NP

Reporter: Tell us about your time on the island. How did you survive?

You: What a difficult experience it was! I hunted goats and found coconuts to eat.

Reporter: Were you ever scared?

You: Yes! What terrible storms we had! But I built a shelter from palm leaves and stayed safe.

Reporter: How were you finally rescued?

You: I lit a fire every day to signal ships. What a happy day it was when I saw the rescue boat!

🧳 Scenario 3: Planning a Survival Trip

  • You and a friend are planning a survival camping trip to an island
  • Discuss what items to bring and why
  • Use need to, should, and island vocabulary

You: We need to make a list of things to bring. First, we should bring a lighter to make fire.

Friend: Good idea. We also need to bring a knife for cutting wood and hunting.

You: What a great idea! What about water? We should bring bottles.

Friend: Yes, and a rope. It can help us build a shelter.

You: We don't have to bring food if we can hunt and find coconuts. What an exciting adventure this will be!
