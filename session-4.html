💬 Idioms

Friendship Idioms

Common English idioms about friendship and relationships. Each includes meaning, example, and Arabic hint.

🤝 Get along with

Meaning: Have a good, friendly relationship with someone

Example: "I get along with my classmates very well."

Arabic: بيتفاهم مع / علاقته كويسة مع

💪 Count on someone

Meaning: Trust and depend on someone

Example: "You can always count on Mona — she never lets you down."

Arabic: تعتمد على حد

💎 A friend in need is a friend indeed

Meaning: A true friend helps you when you have problems

Example: "She helped me when I was sick — a friend in need is a friend indeed."

Arabic: الصاحب وقت الضيق

💛 Through thick and thin

Meaning: In good times and bad times — always loyal

Example: "Best friends stick together through thick and thin."

Arabic: في السراء والضراء

🗡️ Stab someone in the back

Meaning: Betray someone who trusts you

Example: "He told my secret to everyone — he stabbed me in the back."

Arabic: طعنه في ضهره

🧊 Break the ice

Meaning: Do or say something to make people feel comfortable in a new situation

Example: "The teacher played a game to break the ice on the first day."

Arabic: يكسر الحاجز / يفتح الكلام

👀 See eye to eye

Meaning: Agree with someone completely

Example: "We don't always see eye to eye, but we respect each other."

Arabic: متفقين في الرأي

Phase 3 — Grammar

📝 Step 3: Grammar — this / that / these / those

👉 Demonstrative Pronouns & Adjectives

SingularPlural
Near (close to you)thisthese
Far (away from you)thatthose
Tip: Use this/these for things close to you, and that/those for things far away.

💬 As Adjectives (before a noun)

This friend is very kind. / That boy is in my class.

These cookies are for the party. / Those people are my neighbors.

💬 As Pronouns (replace the noun)

This is my best friend Sarah. / That is our favorite cafe.

These are my classmates. / Those were the best days!

⚠ Common Mistakes

❌ "This people are nice." → ✅ "These people are nice." (plural!)

❌ "These is my friend." → ✅ "This is my friend." (singular!)

📞 On the phone: "Hello? Is that you, Mom?" / "This is Ali speaking."

🤝 Introductions: "This is my friend Ahmed." (NOT "He is my friend Ahmed.")

Phase 4 — Reading

📖 Step 4: Reading — What Makes a Good Friend?

Everyone needs good friends. A good friend is someone who helps you feel happy and supported. Good friends usually have things in common with you — they live near you or go to your school.

A good friend cares about you and never tries to make you unhappy. One important quality of a good friend is being honest with you. When you have a problem, a good friend will help you — not laugh at you or ignore you.

A good friend tells the truth in a nice way — not in an angry voice. Words like "You can do it!" from a friend make you feel happy and strong.

Good friends listen to each other, share their things, and spend time together. Being a good friend means being kind, helpful, and caring. If you want to have a good friend, you need to be a good friend too!

Phase 6 — Practice

🎭 Step 6: Roleplay

📞 Scenario 1: Phone Call to a Friend

  • Call your friend to say thank you for helping you
  • Talk about what makes your friendship special
  • Promise to always be good friends

You: Hi! How are you?

Friend: I'm great! What's up?

You: I just wanted to say thank you for helping me with my homework yesterday.

Friend: No problem! That's what friends are for.

You: You are a really kind and supportive friend. I'm lucky to have you!

Friend: Aww, thank you! You're a great friend too.

You: Let's promise to always be good friends.

Friend: I promise! Best friends forever!

👥 Scenario 2: Introducing a New Friend

  • Introduce your new friend to your old friend
  • Describe what your new friend is like
  • Use this/that correctly

You: Hey Ahmed! This is my new friend Mona. She's in my English class.

Ahmed: Hi Mona! Nice to meet you!

Mona: Nice to meet you too!

You: Yes! That is Ahmed. He is very funny and loyal.

Mona: I'm kind and honest. I like helping people!

You: These are my two best friends now!

💬 Scenario 3: Talking About Friend Qualities

  • Discuss what qualities are important in a friend
  • Share a story about your friend being helpful
  • Use personality adjectives

You: What do you think is the most important quality in a friend?

Partner: I think being honest is the most important. What about you?

You: I agree! And being supportive too. My friend always says "You can do it!" when I'm worried.

Partner: That is really nice! My friend is very generous.

You: A good friend is someone who listens to you and tells you the truth in a kind way.

Partner: Exactly! Those are the most important things in friendship.

🟢 Demonstratives

that = singular + far
these = plural + near
those = plural + far
