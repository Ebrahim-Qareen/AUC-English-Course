📖 Step 4: Read & Understand

🌍 Street Food Around the World

Street food is popular in almost every country in the world. In Mexico, you can find spicy tacos sold by vendors on busy streets. The tortillas are fresh, and the fillings are hot and delicious.

In Japan, yakitori is a favorite street food. Vendors grill small pieces of chicken on wooden skewers. The smell of grilled yakitori fills the night markets.

India is famous for samosas — crispy pastries filled with potatoes and peas. You can buy them from stalls on almost every street corner. They are usually served with spicy chutney.

In Greece, gyros is one of the most popular street foods. It is made with grilled lamb or chicken, fresh vegetables, and creamy tzatziki sauce, all wrapped in warm pita bread.

Street food is not just cheap and quick — it also tells you about a country's culture and traditions. Next time you travel, try the local street food. You might discover your new favorite dish!
Did you notice? The passage uses many food adjectives: spicy, fresh, hot, delicious, crispy, grilled, creamy, warm!

🎭 Step 6: Roleplay — Street Food Market

Click each role to see example conversation. Practice with a partner!

🍜 Role 1: The Customer

You want to order food at a street food stall.

  • Ask what food is available
  • Order using "Can I have some..." or "I'd like a..."
  • Ask about ingredients and taste
You: Hi! What do you have today?
Vendor: We have tacos, falafel, and noodles.
You: Can I have some falafel, please?
Vendor: Sure! Would you like any sauce with that?
You: Yes, please! Is it spicy?
Vendor: A little. It's delicious!

🧑‍🍳 Role 2: The Vendor

You sell street food and recommend dishes to customers.

  • Describe your food using adjectives
  • Use "Would you like some...?" to offer
  • Tell customers about ingredients
Customer: What's good today?
You: Try our grilled yakitori! It's hot and delicious.
Customer: Do you have any vegetarian options?
You: Yes! We have some crispy falafel with fresh vegetables.
Customer: That sounds great. I'll have that!

📱 Role 3: The Food Blogger

You are writing about street food for social media.

  • Describe the food using many adjectives
  • Ask the vendor about the recipe
  • Use "This is the most..." / "It tastes..."
You: Wow, this samosa is crispy and delicious!
Vendor: Thank you! It's our most popular dish.
You: How many do you sell every day?
Vendor: About 200! People love them.
You: I can see why! The filling is hot and fresh.

🌍 Role 4: The Tourist

You are visiting a new city and want to try local street food.

  • Ask locals about popular street food
  • Use "What do you recommend?" / "Is there any...?"
  • Describe what you tried
You: Excuse me, what street food is popular here?
Local: You should try the gyros! They're amazing.
You: Is there any vegetarian food?
Local: Yes! The falafel stall on the corner has some great options.
You: How much does a falafel sandwich cost?
Local: About 30 pounds. Very cheap and tasty!

📋 Reference

Cheat Sheet

🟢 Countable vs Uncountable

Countable: taco, egg, burger, samosa
Uncountable: rice, chicken, bread, juice
a/an = countable only
some = both types

🔵 Articles

a/an = first time, not specific
the = specific, already known
Zero article = general
"I love street food." (general)

🟡 some / any

some = positive + offers
any = questions + negatives
"I have some rice."
"Do you have any tacos?"

🔴 How much / How many

How much = uncountable
How many = countable
too much / too many
enough / not enough
