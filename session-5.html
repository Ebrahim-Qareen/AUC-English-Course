Social Media & Connection Idioms

Common English idioms about social media, communication, and staying connected. Each includes meaning, example, and Arabic hint.

📱 Catch up with someone

Meaning: Talk to someone you haven't seen in a while to learn their news

Example: "I need to catch up with my old school friends on WhatsApp."

Arabic: تتواصل مع حد من زمان

🚫 Lose touch

Meaning: Stop communicating with someone over time

Example: "I lost touch with my childhood friends after we moved."

Arabic: انقطع التواصل

📢 Go viral

Meaning: Spread very quickly on the internet to millions of people

Example: "His funny video went viral — it got 10 million views!"

Arabic: انتشر بسرعة على النت

📲 Scroll through

Meaning: Move through content on your phone quickly without focusing

Example: "I spent 2 hours just scrolling through Instagram."

Arabic: بيتصفح من غير تركيز

💣 Drop a bombshell

Meaning: Share shocking or surprising news suddenly

Example: "She dropped a bombshell when she posted that she's moving abroad."

Arabic: فاجأ الكل بخبر صادم

👭 In the loop

Meaning: Kept informed about what's happening

Example: "Keep me in the loop about the group project updates."

Arabic: خليني عارف اللي بيحصل

👻 Ghost someone

Meaning: Suddenly stop replying to someone without explanation

Example: "He ghosted me — he stopped answering all my messages!"

Arabic: اختفى فجأة ومردش

Phase 3 — Grammar

📝 Step 3: Grammar — Past Continuous Tense

⌛ Past Continuous — Formula

Subject + was/were + verb-ing
SubjectHelperExample
I / He / She / Itwas + verb-ingI was reading a book.
We / You / Theywere + verb-ingThey were chatting online.
Tip: Use Past Continuous for actions that were in progress at a specific time in the past.

💬 Examples

Positive: She was chatting with her friend online.

Negative: I wasn't watching TV at 8 pm.

Question: Were you studying when I called?

🔄 Past Continuous + Past Simple (Interruption)

Use Past Continuous for the longer action and Past Simple for the shorter action that interrupted it.

I was reading a book when the phone rang.

We were chatting online when the internet went off.

When = the short action happened.
While = the long action was happening.
"While I was studying, my friend called."

⚠ THE BIG MISTAKE

❌ "I was went to school." → ✅ "I was going to school." (use verb-ing, not past tense!)

❌ "She was cooking when the lights was going off." → ✅ "She was cooking when the lights went off." (interruption = Past Simple!)

❌ "I was reading a book yesterday." (for a completed action) → ✅ "I read a book yesterday." (completed = Past Simple!)

Phase 4 — Reading

📖 Step 4: Reading — Friends on Social Media

Today, many people use social media to talk to friends, share photos, and stay connected. Social media platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp help people find old friends and meet new ones.

However, talking online is not the same as meeting face-to-face. When you see your friends in person, you can understand their feelings better through their voice and body language.

Social media has many advantages. It helps you meet new friends, find old friends, and learn new things from people around the world. New friends can teach you about different cultures and ideas.

But there are some disadvantages too. Social media can take time away from your family and homework. Some people spend too many hours scrolling and forget to do important things.

It is also important to be careful online. You should not share all your personal information with people you don't know well. Not everyone online is who they say they are, so you cannot trust new online friends easily.

Phase 6 — Practice

🎭 Step 6: Roleplay

📱 Scenario 1: Reconnecting Online

  • You found your old primary school friend on social media
  • Send them a message and catch up
  • Talk about a shared memory

You: Hi! Is that you, Sara? It's me from primary school!

Sara: Oh my God! I can't believe it! How are you?

You: I'm great! I was looking through old photos when I saw your profile.

Sara: That's amazing! I was thinking about our old class the other day.

You: Do you remember when we were playing in the school garden? That was so fun!

Sara: Of course! Those were the best days. We should stay in touch!

You: Definitely! Let's meet face-to-face soon.

Sara: I'd love that! I'll send you my number.

👥 Scenario 2: Talking About Social Media Habits

  • Discuss which social media platforms you use
  • Talk about what you do on social media
  • Compare online and face-to-face friendships

You: Which social media platforms do you use?

Classmate: I mostly use Instagram and WhatsApp. What about you?

You: I created an account on Instagram last year. I use it to stay connected with friends.

Classmate: Do you prefer chatting online or meeting face-to-face?

You: I prefer to meet face-to-face because you can understand feelings better.

Classmate: I agree! But social media helps us stay in touch when we're far away.

You: That's true. I reconnected with my old friend through Facebook!

🛡 Scenario 3: Discussing Online Safety

  • Talk about the risks of sharing personal information
  • Give advice about being safe online
  • Use vocabulary from the lesson

You: Mom, can I create an account on a new social media platform?

Parent: Which one? You need to be careful online.

You: It's a platform for sharing photos. My friends use it.

Parent: OK, but remember — never share your personal information with strangers.

You: I know. I'll read the rules carefully before I join.

Parent: Good. And don't spend too much time on it — it can take time from your homework.

You: I promise! I'll only use it to stay in touch with friends I know.

Parent: That's a smart plan. Be careful and enjoy it!

📝 Grammar Rules — Past Continuous

SubjectFormulaExample
I / He / She / Itwas + verb-ingShe was chatting online.
We / You / Theywere + verb-ingThey were sharing photos.
When = short action (Past Simple): "I was reading when she called."
While = long action (Past Continuous): "While I was studying, he arrived."
📋 Reference

Cheat Sheet

🟢 Past Continuous Formula

I/He/She/It + was + verb-ing
We/You/They + were + verb-ing
Negative: was/were + not + verb-ing
Question: Was/Were + subject + verb-ing?

🔵 When vs While

When + Past Simple (short action)
While + Past Continuous (long action)
"I was reading when she called."
"While we were talking, he arrived."
