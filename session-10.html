💬 Idioms

Survival Idioms

Common English idioms about teamwork, survival, and creative thinking. Each includes meaning, example, and Arabic hint.

🎓 Learn the ropes

Meaning: Learn how to do something new

Example: "On the island, everyone had to learn the ropes quickly to survive."

Arabic: يتعلم الأساسيات

🏰 Hold the fort

Meaning: Take care of things while someone is away

Example: "Jack told Tom to hold the fort while he went to find water."

Arabic: يحافظ على الوضع

💪 Pull your weight

Meaning: Do your fair share of work

Example: "Everyone needs to pull their weight if we want to survive."

Arabic: يبذل جهده

🌲 Out of the woods

Meaning: Out of danger or difficulty

Example: "After the ship rescued them, they were finally out of the woods."

Arabic: خرج من المشكلة

👀 Keep an eye out

Meaning: Watch carefully for something

Example: "Keep an eye out for any ships passing by the island."

Arabic: يراقب بحذر

📄 On the same page

Meaning: Agree or understand each other

Example: "Jack made sure everyone was on the same page about the rules."

Arabic: متفقين

💡 Think outside the box

Meaning: Be creative in solving problems

Example: "They had to think outside the box to build shelters from leaves."

Arabic: يفكر بطريقة إبداعية

🚫 Don't + Base Verb

Use Don't + base verb to give warnings, prohibitions, rules, and negative instructions.

PatternExampleUse
Don't + verb!Don't walk alone!Warning
Don't + verb + object!Don't let the fire go out!Rule
Don't + verb + object!Don't drink seawater!Prohibition
Don't + verb!Don't go there!Warning
Don't + verb + object!Don't leave food on the ground!Instruction
Don't + verb + adverb!Don't build in the forest!Instruction

💬 Everyday Examples

Negative imperatives are used in daily life too:

SentenceSituation
Don't be late for class!School
Don't forget your homework!School
Don't worry about the test!Encouragement
Remember: Always use Don't + base verb (not "Not go" or "Don't to walk" or "Doesn't walk"). The subject "you" is hidden/understood.

📖 Reading

Captain Jack and the Island Story

Jack was the captain of a small boat, sailing with his crew and a few passengers. One sunny morning, a sudden storm rolled in. Huge waves rocked the boat until it finally sank. Jack and a group of survivors swam to a desert island. The island had beautiful palm trees, white sand, and a clear blue sea, but they were far from home and needed to stay alive.

As the captain, Jack became the leader. He gathered everyone and said, "We will be ok if we work together. But, we need some rules to stay safe." Jack wrote the rules on a large piece of wood and explained them one by one:

Stay together. "We must work as a team to survive. Don't walk alone!" Jack said firmly.

Keep the fire burning. Jack pointed to the small fire they had started on the beach. "This will keep us warm and safe. Don't let it go out!"

Drink only clean water. Jack showed them a small stream in the east. "This water is fresh. Don't drink seawater; it will make you sick!"

Avoid the south hill. "There are snakes on the south hill," Jack warned. "Don't go there!"

Store food properly. Jack held up some big leaves. "Wrap the fruit and fish in these. Don't leave food on the ground; it will attract animals!"

Build shelters near the beach. Jack explained, "The beach is flat and safe. Don't build in the forest; it's too dark and full of insects!"

Signal for help. Jack encouraged them to use smoke from the fire and to arrange big rocks in an SOS shape on the sand.

The group worked hard, following Jack's rules. Slowly, they learned how to live on the island. One evening, while sitting around the fire, someone saw a light in the sky.

"It's a ship!" shouted one of the passengers. Everyone jumped up, waving and shouting. Thanks to Jack's leadership and their SOS signal, the ship saw them and came to their rescue.

Jack and the group left the island feeling proud of their teamwork and thankful for the captain who kept them safe.

🎭 Roleplay

Roleplay Scenarios

🏝 Scenario 1: Island Leader

  • You are the leader of a group on a desert island
  • Give rules to your group using negative imperatives
  • Your partner plays a group member asking questions

You (Leader): Listen everyone! We need rules to survive. First, don't walk alone at night. It's too dangerous.

Member: What about water? Can we drink from the sea?

You: No! Don't drink seawater! It will make you sick. We found a stream in the east. Use that.

Member: What about food?

You: Don't leave food on the ground! Wrap it in big leaves. And don't go to the south hill — there are snakes there!

Member: You're a great leader. We feel safe with these rules.

🎬 Scenario 2: Survival Movie Discussion

  • Discuss your favorite survival movie with a friend
  • Your partner suggests a movie too
  • Use vocabulary from the session

You: Have you seen Cast Away? It's about a man who survives alone on an island.

Friend: Yes! He talks to a volleyball named Wilson. What about Life of Pi?

You: That's amazing! Pi is stuck on a boat with a tiger. He had to survive by catching fish and collecting rainwater.

Friend: I think Swiss Family Robinson is great too. The family builds a huge treehouse!

You: All these movies teach us that teamwork and creativity are important for survival.

📻 Scenario 3: Rescue Call

  • You found a radio on the island
  • Call for help describing your situation
  • Your partner plays the rescue team

You: Mayday! Mayday! This is a survivor on a desert island. Can anyone hear me?

Rescue: We hear you! What is your situation?

You: Our boat sank in a storm. There are six survivors. We built shelters on the beach and we have a fire burning for signal.

Rescue: Stay calm. Can you see any landmarks?

You: Yes, there are palm trees and a hill to the south. We arranged rocks in an SOS shape on the sand. Please hurry!

Rescue: We're sending a ship your way. Don't leave the beach! Stay near your signal fire.

Quick Cheat Sheet

🚫 Grammar Rules

Don't + base verb = negative imperative
Use for warnings, rules, prohibitions.
"Don't walk alone!"
"Don't drink seawater!"

💬 Useful Expressions

Don't worry!
Don't forget...
Don't be late!
Don't give up!

🗣 Speaking Rescue Phrases

"I think the most important rule is..."
"In my opinion, you should never..."
"If I were on an island, I would..."

⚠ Common Mistakes

✗ "Not go there" → ✓ "Don't go there"
✗ "Don't to walk" → ✓ "Don't walk"
✗ "Doesn't walk" → ✓ "Don't walk"
