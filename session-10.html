📋 Rule: Don't + base verb

We use Don't + base verb to give warnings, instructions, rules, and prohibitions.

SituationNegative Imperative
The floor is wetDon't walk on the floor!
Fire is dangerousDon't touch it!
Food is not freshDon't eat it!
River is deepDon't swim there!
Seawater is saltyDon't drink seawater!
Snakes on the hillDon't go there!
Tip: Use negative imperatives to give rules and warnings. They are short, clear, and direct!

📖 Reading

Captain Jack and the Island

Jack was the captain of a small boat, sailing with his crew and a few passengers. One sunny morning, a sudden storm rolled in. Huge waves rocked the boat until it finally sank. Jack and a group of survivors swam to a desert island.

The island had beautiful palm trees, white sand, and a clear blue sea, but they were far from home and needed to stay alive.

As the captain, Jack became the leader. He gathered everyone and said, “We will be ok if we work together. But, we need some rules to stay safe.”

Jack wrote the rules on a large piece of wood:

1. Stay together. “Don't walk alone!”

2. Keep the fire burning. “Don't let it go out!”

3. Drink only clean water. “Don't drink seawater; it will make you sick!”

4. Avoid the south hill. “Don't go there!” (snakes)

5. Store food properly. “Don't leave food on the ground!”

6. Build shelters near the beach. “Don't build in the forest!”

The group worked hard, following Jack's rules. One evening, someone saw a light — “It's a ship!” Thanks to Jack's leadership and their SOS signal, the ship saw them and came to their rescue.

🎭 Roleplay

Roleplay Scenarios

🌊 Scenario 1: Island Leader

  • You are the leader of the group on a deserted island
  • Give 5 survival rules using negative imperatives
  • Explain why each rule is important

You (Leader): Listen everyone! Don't walk alone in the forest — there might be dangerous animals.

Crew: Ok, what else?

You: Don't drink seawater! It will make you very sick. Only drink from the stream.

Crew: What about food?

You: Don't leave food on the ground. Wrap it in leaves. And don't go to the south hill — there are snakes!

🎬 Scenario 2: Movie Discussion

  • Discuss your favorite survival movie with a friend
  • Describe what the characters did to survive
  • Use vocabulary: survive, rescue, shelter, crew, teamwork

You: Have you watched Cast Away? The main character survives alone on an island!

Friend: Yes! He talks to a volleyball named Wilson. What a lonely experience!

You: He had to learn the ropes of survival — finding food, making fire, and building shelter.

Friend: The rescue at the end was amazing. He never gave up!

🏕 Scenario 3: Forest Safety

  • You are a guide teaching someone forest safety rules
  • Give warnings using “Don't...”
  • Use idioms: keep an eye out, out of the woods, think outside the box

You (Guide): Welcome to the forest! First rule: don't wander off the path.

Hiker: What about animals?

You: Keep an eye out for tracks and strange noises. Don't approach wild animals!

Hiker: What if I get lost?

You: Don't panic! Stay calm and think outside the box. Start a fire for warmth and as a signal.

📋 Cheat Sheet

Quick Reference

🚫 Negative Imperative

Don't + base verb (+ object)
"Don't walk alone!"
"Don't drink seawater!"
"Don't leave food on the ground!"

⚠ When to Use

Warnings, rules, instructions, prohibitions
"Don't touch the fire!"
"Don't go near the snakes!"

🌊 Key Vocabulary

captain, crew, storm, survivors, shelter, stream, signal, SOS, rescue, teamwork, wrap, palm trees, fire, snakes

🗣 Speaking Rescue Phrases

"Don't..." • "You must..." • "It's important to..." • "Be careful of..." • "Make sure you..."

❌ Common Mistakes

✗ "Don't to walk" → ✓ "Don't walk"
✗ "Don't walks" → ✓ "Don't walk"
✗ "Not drink water" → ✓ "Don't drink water"
