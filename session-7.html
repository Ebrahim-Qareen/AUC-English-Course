💬 Idioms

Negative Emotions Idioms

English idioms for expressing sadness, frustration, and other negative feelings. Each includes meaning, example, and Arabic hint.

😔 Feel down

Meaning: Feel sad or unhappy

Example: "I've been feeling down since my friend moved away."

Arabic: حاسس بالزعل / مش مبسوط

😠 Fed up with

Meaning: Very annoyed and tired of something that keeps happening

Example: "I'm fed up with the traffic every morning!"

Arabic: زهقت من / طفشت من

💢 Hit the roof

Meaning: Become extremely angry suddenly

Example: "My dad hit the roof when he saw my phone bill."

Arabic: اتجنن / اتنرفز جداً

😭 Down in the dumps

Meaning: Feeling very sad and depressed

Example: "She's been down in the dumps since she failed the exam."

Arabic: محبط / مكتئب

😰 At the end of my rope

Meaning: So stressed or frustrated that you can't handle anymore

Example: "With exams and work, I'm at the end of my rope!"

Arabic: وصلت لآخري / مش قادر أكمل

💔 Have a heavy heart

Meaning: Feel very sad about something

Example: "I left my hometown with a heavy heart."

Arabic: قلبي تقيل / حزين من جوّا

🧊 Give someone the cold shoulder

Meaning: Ignore someone on purpose; treat them in an unfriendly way

Example: "After our argument, she gave me the cold shoulder for a week."

Arabic: طنشه / عامله ببرود

Phase 3 — Grammar

📝 Step 3: Grammar — need to / want to

📋 need to / want to — Formula

Subject + need/want + to + base verb
Subjectneed towant to
I / You / We / Theyneed to + verbwant to + verb
He / She / Itneeds to + verbwants to + verb
need to = something necessary or important.
want to = something you desire or wish for.

💬 Examples with Emotions

"I feel tired. I need to take a break."

"She is sad. She wants to talk to someone."

"He feels lonely. He wants to make new friends."

"We are stressed. We need to review everything."

💡 Needs vs Wants

Needs = things important for a safe, healthy life: water, bread, house, blanket

Wants = extra things we like but don't need: car, TV, smartphone, ice cream, vacation

Think about it: Is a phone a need or a want? In 2025, many people feel they need a phone, but humans survived without them!

⚠ THE BIG MISTAKE

❌ "I need taking a break." → ✅ "I need to take a break."

❌ "She wants making friends." → ✅ "She wants to make friends."

❌ "He need to study." → ✅ "He needs to study." (He/She/It = needs)

Phase 4 — Reading

📖 Step 4: Reading — The Surprising Upsides of Bad Moods

Many people today feel "mood shame" — they feel bad about feeling bad. They think they should always be happy, and when they are not, they try to hide their negative emotions. But is this really the right thing to do?

Research shows that bad feelings can actually help us. They can tell us what is important to us. For example, if you feel angry about something, it might mean that thing really matters to you. If you feel worried about an exam, it shows you care about your grades.

The Italian writer Ilaria Gaspari says that being emotional is not a weakness — it means you are alive. When we feel emotions, we are connecting with the world around us.

Studies show that people who accept their feelings — both positive and negative — are usually healthier and happier than people who try to push their bad feelings away.

So what should we do when we feel bad? Experts say we should try to understand why we feel this way, instead of ignoring the feeling. Ask yourself: "Why am I feeling this?" and "What do I need to do about it?" Emotions are not clearly positive or negative — they are all part of being human.

Phase 5 — Output

🗣 Step 5: Speaking Practice

🎯 1-Minute Challenge

Talk about a time you felt worried or anxious. What happened and what did you do? Use "need to" and "want to"!

Example: "Last week, I was very worried about my English exam. I needed to study harder, but I also wanted to relax..."

💬 Useful Expressions for Talking About Feelings

"I felt... because..."
Explain the emotion and reason
"I needed to... so I..."
Talk about what you did
"I was worried about..."
Describe your concern
"It made me feel..."
Describe the emotional impact
"I wanted to... but..."
Talk about conflicting desires
"Now I understand that..."

Phase 6 — Practice

🎭 Step 6: Roleplay

😔 Scenario 1: Helping a Depressed Friend

  • Your friend is feeling depressed after getting bad grades
  • Give advice using "need to" and "want to"
  • Be supportive and understanding

You: Hey, are you OK? You look sad today.

Friend: I got bad grades on my exam. I feel so depressed.

You: I'm sorry to hear that. But you need to remember — one exam doesn't define you.

Friend: I know, but I'm ashamed. I studied so hard.

You: You need to talk to your teacher and ask for help. Do you want to study together next time?

Friend: Really? You would do that?

You: Of course! You also need to be kinder to yourself. Everyone makes mistakes.

Friend: Thank you. I feel better already. I want to try harder next time.

😠 Scenario 2: Dealing with Anger

  • You are angry because someone was rude to you
  • Talk to a friend about how you feel
  • Discuss what you need to do

You: I'm so angry right now! Someone was very rude to me in class.

Friend: What happened? Tell me about it.

You: They made fun of my English in front of everyone. I felt ashamed.

Friend: That's terrible. You need to take some deep breaths first.

You: You're right. I don't want to say something I'll regret.

Friend: Do you want to talk to the teacher about it?

You: Yes, I think I need to. It's not OK to be rude to people.

Friend: I agree. And remember, your English is great! Don't let anyone make you feel bad.

😰 Scenario 3: Feeling Anxious Before a Presentation

  • You have a monologue assessment tomorrow
  • You are very anxious about speaking in front of the class
  • Ask for advice and support

You: I'm really anxious about my monologue tomorrow. I don't want to forget my words.

Friend: I understand. What do you need to do to prepare?

You: I need to practice more, but I'm too nervous to even start.

Friend: OK, first you need to take a deep breath. Then, do you want to practice with me?

You: That would be great! I want to feel more confident.

Friend: Remember, everyone is nervous before presentations. It's normal!

You: You're right. I need to stop worrying and start practicing.

Friend: That's the spirit! You're going to do great.
