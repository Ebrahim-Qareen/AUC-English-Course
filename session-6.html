What are Adverbs of Degree?

They work like a volume dial for adjectives — they tell us HOW MUCH.

Structure: Subject + Verb + Adverb + Adjective

🟢 very / really = A LOT (no problem!)

Use when the feeling is strong but OK.

✅ The food is very delicious!

✅ I am really happy to see you!

✅ She feels very grateful for the help.

✅ We are really excited about the trip!

🔴 too = MORE THAN ENOUGH (there is a problem!)

Use when something is excessive and causes a problem.

❌ The bag is too heavy. I can't carry it. (problem!)

❌ The room is too noisy. I can't study. (problem!)

❌ The coffee is too hot. I can't drink it. (problem!)

⚠ THE BIG MISTAKE

In Arabic, "جداً" can mean both "very" and "too" — but in English they are DIFFERENT!

❌ WRONG

"I am too happy to see you!"

(Happy is NOT a problem!)

✅ CORRECT

"I am very happy to see you!"

(No problem = use very/really)

Remember: TOO = PROBLEM!

📖 Step 4: Read & Understand

🌟 The Importance of Happiness

Happiness is not just a feeling — it is a powerful force that can change your life. Scientists have found that happy people are healthier, live longer, and have better relationships.

When you feel positive emotions like gratitude, excitement, and hope, your body releases chemicals that reduce stress and make you feel calm. This is why doctors say that happiness is good for both your mind and your body.

Research shows that grateful people sleep better, have more energy, and are more creative. Being thankful for small things — like a good meal, a kind word, or time with friends — can make a big difference.

One simple way to be happier is to focus on positive things every day. You can keep a "positivity box" — write down good moments on small papers and put them in a box. When you feel sad, open the box and read them!

Happiness is also connected to helping others. When you do something kind for someone, you feel good too. Scientists call this the "helper's high." So being kind doesn't just help others — it helps YOU feel happier!
Did you notice? The passage uses many positive feeling words: happiness, gratitude, excitement, hope, grateful, thankful, kind. These are all from your vocabulary list!

🎤 1-Minute Challenge

Set a timer. Speak for 60 seconds without stopping:

"Talk about 3 things that make you feel happy. Use 'very' or 'really' — and explain WHY each one makes you feel that way."

🛡 Rescue Phrases

"I feel really... when..." "It makes me very... because..." "I'm so grateful for..." "What I love most is..." "That's why I feel..."

💬 Pronunciation Tips

grateful — GRAY-ful (not GRAT-ful)

excited — ik-SY-tid (stress on 2nd syllable)

confident — KON-fi-dent (stress on 1st)

peaceful — PEES-ful (long 'ee' sound)

cheerful — CHEER-ful (rhymes with "ear")

🎭 Step 6: Roleplay — Sharing Positive Feelings

Click each role to see guiding points and example conversation. Practice with a partner!

😊 Role 1: The Good News Sharer

You have exciting news and want to share your positive feelings.

  • Start with your exciting news
  • Describe how you feel using 3+ feeling words
  • Use very/really correctly (not too!)
  • Explain why this makes you feel this way
You: Guess what! I just got accepted to the AUC summer program!
Friend: Wow! That's amazing! How do you feel?
You: I'm really excited about it! And I feel very proud of myself because I worked so hard.
Friend: You should be! Are you nervous too?
You: A little, but mostly I'm very hopeful about the future. I'm also really grateful for my family's support.

🤝 Role 2: The Supportive Listener

Your friend shares good news. React and ask about their feelings.

  • Show you're happy for your friend
  • Ask questions about their feelings
  • Share a similar experience
  • Use feeling words in your responses
Friend: I passed my driving test today!
You: That's wonderful! You must be very proud!
Friend: Yes! I feel so confident now.
You: I remember when I passed mine. I was really excited too! How does your family feel?
Friend: They're over the moon! My mom is very grateful because now I can drive her to the market.

🎙 Role 3: The Happiness Interviewer

Interview your partner about what makes them happy.

  • Ask: "What makes you feel [feeling word]?"
  • Ask follow-up questions about WHY
  • Compare their answers to your own feelings
  • Use at least 5 different feeling words
You: What makes you feel peaceful?
Partner: Sitting by the Nile at sunset. It's very relaxing.
You: That sounds lovely! And what are you most grateful for?
Partner: My friends. They always make me feel cheerful even when I'm stressed.
You: I feel the same way. What makes you feel confident?
Partner: Preparing well for exams. When I study hard, I feel really confident about the test.

Email Task

My family is very grateful for the teachers who helped me. I'm also really hopeful about improving my English this year.

What about you? What made you feel happy this week? I'd love to hear your good news!

✏ Step 8: Writing — Paragraph About Positive Emotions

💡 Writing Task

Write a paragraph (60-80 words) about the advantages of positive emotions. Explain how feeling happy, grateful, or hopeful can improve our lives.

Model:

Positive emotions have many advantages. When we feel grateful, we notice the good things in life and feel more peaceful. Being hopeful helps us work harder toward our goals. Excited people are more creative and solve problems better. Also, when we feel cheerful, we make others around us happy too. I think everyone should try to focus on positive feelings because they make life really beautiful.
