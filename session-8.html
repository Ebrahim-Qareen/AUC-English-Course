💬 Idioms

Money & Budgeting Idioms

Common English idioms about money, spending, and saving. Each includes meaning, example, and Arabic hint.

💰 Save up for

Meaning: Keep money over time to buy something later

Example: "I'm saving up for a new laptop."

Arabic: بيوفّر فلوس عشان

🏦 Break the bank

Meaning: Cost too much money; very expensive

Example: "That restaurant won't break the bank — it's very affordable."

Arabic: يكلف كتير أوي / غالي

💸 Pay through the nose

Meaning: Pay much more than something is worth

Example: "We paid through the nose for parking at the airport."

Arabic: دفع فلوس كتير فوق اللزوم

💵 Make ends meet

Meaning: Have just enough money to pay for basic needs

Example: "With rising prices, it's hard to make ends meet."

Arabic: بالعافية بيكفّي مصاريفه

🤑 A rip-off

Meaning: Something that costs much more than it should

Example: "50 pounds for a coffee? That's a rip-off!"

Arabic: نصب / غالي أوي

💲 Worth every penny

Meaning: Something that is good value — you're happy you paid for it

Example: "This course was worth every penny — I learned so much!"

Arabic: يستاهل كل قرش

✂️ Cut back on

Meaning: Reduce spending or use less of something

Example: "I need to cut back on eating out to save money."

Arabic: يقلل من / يوفّر في

📝 Grammar

should / shouldn't / have to / don't have to

📊 Grammar Slides

1 / 1

✅ should = Advice (Good idea)

Use should to give advice or say something is a good idea. It is NOT a rule.

SubjectVerbExample
I / You / We / Theyshould + base verbYou should save money every month.
He / She / Itshould + base verbShe should make a budget.
Remember: "should" NEVER changes! No "shoulds" or "should to". Always: should + base verb.

❌ shouldn't = Advice Against (Bad idea)

Use shouldn't to say something is a bad idea.

SubjectVerbExample
I / You / We / Theyshouldn't + base verbYou shouldn't spend all your salary.
He / She / Itshouldn't + base verbHe shouldn't borrow money he can't pay back.

🚨 have to / has to = Obligation (Rule / Law)

Use have to when something is required — a rule, a law, or necessary.

SubjectVerbExample
I / You / We / Theyhave to + base verbWe have to pay our bills every month.
He / She / Ithas to + base verbShe has to pay back her loan.
THE BIG DIFFERENCE: "should" = advice (your choice) vs "have to" = obligation (no choice, it's required).

👌 don't have to / doesn't have to = No Obligation

Use don't have to when something is NOT necessary — you can choose.

SubjectVerbExample
I / You / We / Theydon't have to + base verbYou don't have to tip at this restaurant.
He / She / Itdoesn't have to + base verbShe doesn't have to pay for parking — it's free.
Don't confuse! "shouldn't" = bad idea (don't do it) vs "don't have to" = not necessary (you can if you want).

📖 Reading

How to Be Smart with Money

Being smart with money doesn't mean you need to be rich. It means understanding how to manage what you have. Many young people start earning money but don't know how to handle it well. Here are some key strategies:

1. Create a Budget
A budget is a plan that shows how much money you earn and how much you spend. It helps you see where your money goes and find ways to save. Start by writing down your income and all your expenses each month.

2. Save Regularly
You should save some money every month, even if it's a small amount. Don't wait until you have extra cash — make saving a habit from the start. Try to save at least 10–20% of your income.

3. Build an Emergency Fund
An emergency fund is money you keep for unexpected situations, like a medical bill or a car repair. Having this fund can help you avoid using credit cards or taking loans in a crisis.

4. Avoid Unnecessary Debt
Try not to borrow money for things you don't really need. If you use a credit card, pay the full amount each month so you don't build up debt.

5. Learn About Investing
Investing means putting your money into something that can grow over time, like stocks or a savings account with good interest. It takes time, but it can help you build wealth for the future.

Adapted from: Mishra, K. (2024). The Financial Express; mlc.com.au

🎭 Roleplay

Roleplay Scenarios

💸 Scenario 1: Friend Who Spends Too Much

  • Your friend spends all their salary on shopping
  • They have no savings and use credit cards
  • Give them advice using should/shouldn't

You: Hey, I noticed you bought a lot of new stuff this month. Are you saving any money?

Friend: Not really. I just like buying things. I'll save next month.

You: I think you should make a budget. You shouldn't spend everything you earn.

Friend: But I don't have to save right now. I'm young!

You: Actually, you should start saving now. What if there's an emergency? You don't have to save a lot — even a small amount helps.

Friend: You're right. I should cut back on shopping and save up for important things.

💳 Scenario 2: Planning a Group Trip

  • You're planning a trip with friends
  • Some want an expensive hotel, others are on a budget
  • Discuss what you have to pay for vs what's optional

You: So, for the trip, we have to book a hotel and buy bus tickets. Those are the main costs.

Friend 1: I think we should get a nice hotel. We deserve it!

Friend 2: I'm on a budget. We don't have to stay at an expensive hotel. A clean, simple one is fine.

You: Good point. We should find something that's not too expensive. We also don't have to eat at restaurants every day — we can cook sometimes.

Friend 1: OK, we should make a budget for the trip so we all agree.

🏦 Scenario 3: Asking About a Bank Loan

  • You want to take a loan for a car
  • Ask the bank about fees and what you have to do
  • Practice using have to, don't have to, should

You: Hello, I'd like to ask about car loans. What do I have to do to get one?

Bank: You have to fill out an application and show proof of income. You also have to pay a small processing fee.

You: Do I have to make a down payment?

Bank: You don't have to, but you should. A down payment means smaller monthly payments.

You: What about insurance?

Bank: You have to have car insurance — it's the law. You should compare prices to get the best deal.

Quick Cheat Sheet

✅ should (Advice)

Subject + should + base verb
"You should save money."
"She should make a budget."

❌ shouldn't (Bad idea)

Subject + shouldn't + base verb
"You shouldn't waste money."
"He shouldn't take a loan."

🚨 have to (Obligation)

I/You/We/They + have to
He/She/It + has to
"We have to pay bills."

👌 don't have to (Not necessary)

I/You/We/They + don't have to
He/She/It + doesn't have to
"You don't have to tip."
