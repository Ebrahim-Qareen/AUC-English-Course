📖 Reading

Clothes for Different Situations

People wear different clothes for different situations. When you go to work, you usually wear something formal — like a suit and tie, or a blouse and skirt. But when you are at home or with friends, you wear something more casual and comfortable, like a t-shirt and jeans.

In cold weather, you need warm clothes: a coat, a scarf, boots, and maybe mittens. In summer, people wear light clothes like shorts, sandals, and sleeveless tops.

For a party or special occasion, you might choose a nice dress or a stylish outfit. Some people love following fashion trends, while others prefer to be comfortable and wear what they like.

When you go shopping for clothes, it's important to try things on. Sometimes a medium size fits you better than a large. And the best clothes are the ones that fit like a glove!

🎭 Roleplay

Roleplay Scenarios

🛍 Scenario 1: Shopping for Clothes

  • You are shopping for new clothes
  • Ask the shop assistant about sizes and colors
  • Use: dress, jeans, pair of, try on, fit

You: Excuse me, do you have these jeans in a medium?

Assistant: Let me check... Yes, here you go!

You: Can I try them on? I also need a pair of shoes to match.

Assistant: Of course! The fitting room is over there. What size shoes?

You: Size 42, please. And do you have any nice shirts?

👗 Scenario 2: What to Wear?

  • Your friend needs outfit advice for a job interview
  • Suggest formal clothes and explain why
  • Use idioms: dressed to the nines, fit like a glove

Friend: I have a job interview tomorrow. What should I wear?

You: You should dress to the nines! Wear a nice suit with a tie.

Friend: What about my shoes?

You: Wear your black leather shoes — they fit like a glove! And make sure your clothes are clean and ironed.

☀ Scenario 3: Packing for a Trip

  • You are helping someone pack for a beach holiday
  • Discuss clothes for different weather and activities
  • Use plural nouns correctly

You: Don't forget to pack your shorts and sandals!

Friend: What about if it gets cold at night?

You: Bring a sweater and a pair of jeans, just in case. Oh, and don't forget your sunglasses!

Friend: Good idea. Should I bring sneakers?

You: Yes, you need sneakers for hiking. And pack two pairs of socks!

📋 Cheat Sheet

Quick Reference

📚 Plural Rules

+s: shirt→shirts
+es: dress→dresses
y→ies: accessory→accessories
f→ves: scarf→scarves
Irregular: foot→feet, child→children

⚠ Always Plural

jeans, pants, shorts, scissors, glasses, pajamas
Count with “a pair of”
“I bought two pairs of jeans.”

👕 Key Vocabulary

t-shirt, jeans, dress, blouse, suit, tie, sweater, coat, boots, sandals, sneakers, scarf, mittens, skirt, pajamas, outfit, accessories

🗣 Useful Phrases

“I usually wear...” • “For formal events...” • “A pair of...” • “It fits like a glove!” • “I prefer comfortable clothes.”
