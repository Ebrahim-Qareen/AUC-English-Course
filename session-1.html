🎂 Make a wish

Meaning: Think of something you want before blowing out birthday candles

Example: "Close your eyes and make a wish before you blow out the candles!"

Arabic: اتمنّى أمنية

🎉 Have a blast

Meaning: Have a very fun and exciting time

Example: "We had a blast at Sara's birthday party last night!"

Arabic: انبسطنا جداً

🎁 The icing on the cake

Meaning: Something extra good that makes a situation even better

Example: "The surprise gift was the icing on the cake at my birthday."

Arabic: الحاجة اللي كملت الفرحة

🎈 Over the hill

Meaning: Getting old (used humorously)

Example: "Don't say I'm over the hill — I'm only 30!"

Arabic: كبر في السن (بشكل هزلي)

🎊 Many happy returns

Meaning: A formal way to say "Happy Birthday" — wishing many more birthdays

Example: "Many happy returns of the day, Grandma!"

Arabic: عقبال مية سنة

🥳 Paint the town red

Meaning: Go out and celebrate in a big, exciting way

Example: "It's my birthday — let's paint the town red tonight!"

Arabic: نطلع ونحتفل بجد

🕯️ Blow out the candles

Meaning: A birthday tradition — blow air to put out candles on a cake

Example: "Make a wish and blow out the candles!"

Arabic: اطفي الشمع

🟢 "will" — Spontaneous Decisions, Predictions & Promises

Use will + base verb when you decide NOW, predict, or promise.

Structure: Subject + will + base verb

✅ I will invite my best friends to the party.

✅ She will bake a big chocolate cake.

✅ We won't forget to buy balloons.

✅ Will you come to my birthday party?

🇪🇬 Arabic: "will" = هـ + المضارع (هروح / هعمل) — spontaneous future

🔵 "is going to" — Pre-Made Plans & Evidence-Based Predictions

Use am/is/are + going to + base verb when you already planned before.

Structure: Subject + am/is/are + going to + base verb

✅ I am going to have a birthday party on Saturday.

✅ She is going to bake a chocolate cake.

✅ They are going to decorate the room with balloons.

✅ He isn't going to come. He is busy.

🇪🇬 Arabic: "is going to" = ناوي / ناوية (pre-planned) vs "will" = هـ (spontaneous)

⚠ THE BIG MISTAKE

Students often confuse "will" and "going to" — they are NOT the same!

✅ will (just decided NOW)

"Oh! I'll bring some drinks."

(spontaneous — decided this moment)

🔵 going to (planned BEFORE)

"I'm going to bring drinks."

(pre-planned — decided earlier)

❌ "I will to go" → ✅ "I will go" (no "to" after will)

❌ "She will goes" → ✅ "She will go" (base verb, no -s)

❌ "I going to have" → ✅ "I am going to have" (need am/is/are)

Did you notice? The invitation uses "will" — "There will be fun games." Look for more examples of future tense in the text!

🌍 The Birthday Party — Cultural Passage

Everyone loves to celebrate a birthday with friends and family members. It's part of most cultures, but not everyone celebrates the same way. There are different things that can make a cultural celebration like a birthday party special and fun. What are some of those things? Special foods, music, and games can make a cultural celebration special and fun.

Some cultures have cake to celebrate a birthday. What kind of food do you eat at your birthday party? In a lot of cultures, there is a special song or kind of music; for example, the Happy Birthday song is popular in many countries around the world.

Birthday games are another really common thing to do at birthday parties in many different cultures. Playing hide-and-seek, pin the tail on the donkey, and hitting a pinata are just some of the more popular ones. What kinds of games do you play at your birthday party?

🌟 The Big Dreamer

  • You are planning the BIGGEST birthday party EVER
  • Share all your crazy plans using "going to"
  • Use: "I'm going to invite 200 people!", "We're going to have a DJ!"
  • React to everything with excitement
Example:
A: "So what are your plans for your birthday?"
B: "Oh, it's going to be AMAZING! I'm going to invite everyone I know!"
A: "Wow, really? Where will it be?"
B: "I'm going to rent the biggest hall in the city! There will be a DJ, a chocolate fountain..."
A: "That sounds expensive!"
B: "I don't care! I'm going to have the best birthday ever!"

🤔 The Curious Questioner

  • Ask about your friend's birthday plans
  • Use questions with "will" and "going to"
  • Use: "Will there be music?", "Are you going to invite Sara?"
  • Show interest in every answer
Example:
A: "Hey, I heard you're having a birthday party!"
B: "Yes! It's going to be on Saturday!"
A: "Will there be cake? What flavor?"
B: "Of course! I'm going to order a chocolate cake."
A: "Are you going to invite Omar? Will there be games?"
B: "Everyone is invited! And yes, we're going to play lots of games!"

🧐 The Party Judge

  • Your friend tells you about their party plans
  • Give opinions: "That will be fun!" or "That won't work..."
  • Suggest better ideas using "will" and "going to"
  • Be helpful but honest
Example:
A: "I'm going to have my party at the park."
B: "Hmm, that won't work if it rains..."
A: "Oh... you're right. What do you think?"
B: "The Student Center will be better! It won't rain inside!"
A: "Good idea! I'm going to book it tomorrow."
B: "That will be perfect! Everyone will love it."

🙅 The "Won't" Person

  • Your friend invites you to a party but you say NO to everything
  • Use "won't" for all your excuses
  • Use: "I won't come because...", "I won't eat cake because..."
  • Eventually your friend convinces you!
Example:
A: "Come to my birthday party on Saturday!"
B: "I won't come. I'll be too tired."
A: "But there will be amazing food!"
B: "I won't eat cake. I'm on a diet."
A: "We're going to play your favorite games..."
B: "Well... okay, maybe I'll come for just one hour!"
A: "Great! You won't regret it!"
